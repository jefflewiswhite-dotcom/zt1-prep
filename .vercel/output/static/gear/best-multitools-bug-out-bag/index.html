What Makes a Good BOB Multitool?

Essential tools:

  • Pliers (needle-nose preferred)
  • Wire cutters
  • Knife blade (plain edge most versatile)
  • Screwdrivers (Phillips and flat)
  • Can/bottle opener
  • File

Nice to have:

  • Scissors
  • Saw (wood)
  • Awl
  • Wire stripper

Build quality matters: A multitool that breaks or malfunctions is worse than no tool at all. Stick with reputable brands.

Note

The best multitool is the one you’ll actually carry. A heavy, bulky tool left at home is useless. Consider weight and size alongside features.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a multitool enough, or do I need a separate knife?
For most emergency scenarios, a multitool is sufficient. If you expect heavy cutting tasks (processing firewood, building shelter from scratch), a fixed blade is more capable. Many people carry both—multitool for utility, fixed blade for serious cutting.
Leatherman vs. Victorinox—which is better?
Both make excellent tools. Leatherman tends to have better pliers and is more repair-focused. Victorinox has excellent knives and scissors. For BOB use, Leatherman's pliers usually win out.
Are cheap multitools worth buying?
For occasional light use, budget tools work. For emergency preparedness where failure matters, invest in Leatherman, Victorinox, or SOG. A broken tool when you need it is worse than no tool.
How do I maintain my multitool?
Keep it clean and lightly oiled. Work the hinges periodically. Clean and dry it after wet use. Sharpen blades as needed. A well-maintained Leatherman can last decades.
Can I fly with a multitool?
Not in carry-on luggage (TSA will confiscate it). Pack multitools in checked baggage only. For travel, consider a bladeless tool like the Leatherman Style PS.
