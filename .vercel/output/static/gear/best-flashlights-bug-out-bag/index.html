What Makes a Good BOB Flashlight?

For emergency use, prioritize:

Durability — Your light may get dropped, crushed, or soaked. It needs to survive.

Battery availability — CR123A or AA batteries are available everywhere. 18650s offer better performance but require planning.

Brightness — 200-500 lumens handles most needs. More is nice but drains batteries faster.

Reliability — Name-brand lights with quality switches and seals. Your life may depend on it.

Runtime — Look for low/medium modes that extend battery life for extended use.

Note

Lumens measure total light output, not how far light travels. A 500-lumen light with good focus can outperform a 1,000-lumen light with wide flood.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many lumens do I actually need?
For most BOB use, 200-500 lumens is plenty. Higher lumens drain batteries faster. Prioritize reliability and battery life over maximum brightness.
Should I get a rechargeable flashlight?
Rechargeable is convenient for everyday use, but for emergency preparedness, having spare batteries you can stockpile is valuable. Ideally, get a light that's rechargeable but can also use replaceable batteries.
How many extra batteries should I pack?
At minimum, one full reload (2 CR123As or 1 18650). For extended preparedness, 2-3 reloads. Rotate batteries yearly.
Are cheap flashlights from Amazon okay?
Sometimes. Unknown brands often lie about specifications and may fail when you need them. Stick with established brands (Streamlight, Fenix, ThruNite, Olight, Coast) for reliability.
Does the color of light matter?
Standard white/cool white is fine for most use. Some lights offer red mode which preserves night vision—nice to have but not essential.
