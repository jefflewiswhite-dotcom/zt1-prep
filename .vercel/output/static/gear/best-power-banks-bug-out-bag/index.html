How Much Capacity Do You Need?

Quick math for phone charging:

  • Modern smartphones have ~3,000-4,500mAh batteries
  • A 10,000mAh power bank provides 2-3 full charges
  • A 20,000mAh power bank provides 4-5 full charges
  • Actual output is ~60-70% of rated capacity (energy loss)

For most 72-hour BOBs: 10,000mAh is the sweet spot—enough for several charges without excessive weight.

Note

Capacity alone doesn’t tell the whole story. Quality cells, efficient circuitry, and output wattage matter. A cheap 20,000mAh bank may perform worse than a quality 10,000mAh option.

Solar Charging: Worth It?

Many preppers consider solar panels to recharge their power banks indefinitely.

FactorReality
Charging speedSlow—full day of sun for partial charge
ReliabilityWeather dependent
Weight/bulkAdds significant pack weight
Best use caseBase camp, vehicle kit, long-term scenarios

Our take: For 72-hour BOBs, a well-charged power bank is more practical than solar. Solar makes sense for extended scenarios or as a supplemental charging method.

What to Charge (Priority Order)

When power is limited, prioritize:

  1. Phone — Communication, GPS, information
  2. Headlamp/flashlight (if rechargeable) — Essential for night navigation
  3. Emergency radio (if rechargeable) — Information gathering
  4. GPS device (if separate) — Navigation backup

What NOT to waste power on: tablets, laptops, entertainment devices (unless they serve critical functions).

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a power bank hold its charge?
Quality power banks lose about 1-2% per month when not in use. A full charge should last 6+ months sitting idle. Check and top off quarterly for peace of mind.
Can I bring power banks on airplanes?
Yes, but in carry-on only (not checked baggage). TSA limits to 100Wh per battery, which includes most power banks up to about 27,000mAh. Check current regulations before flying.
Should I get a power bank with solar built-in?
Generally no. Built-in solar panels are too small to charge meaningfully. You're better off with a separate, larger solar panel that can charge your power bank efficiently.
USB-A or USB-C output?
USB-C with Power Delivery charges modern devices faster. If your phone supports it, USB-C is worth having. USB-A works with everything but charges slower.
Do I need a power bank if I have a hand-crank radio?
They serve different purposes. A hand-crank radio charges itself slowly for radio use. A power bank charges your phone (much larger battery) much faster. For phone charging, a power bank is far more practical.
